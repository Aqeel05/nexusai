# OMNIMIND — The Nexus for All of AI

**Project plan · v1.0 · April 2026**

---

## 1. The Name

**OMNIMIND** (domain target: `omnimind.ai` / `omnimind.co`)

**Why this name works:**
- **Omni-** (Latin *omnis* = "all") immediately signals totality — every AI, one place
- **-mind** grounds it in intelligence, not just "tools" — elevates the brand above listicle directories
- Two syllables, hard consonants (M, N, D) — phonetically sticky and memorable
- Visually clean: one word, no punctuation, works as lockup and wordmark
- Semantically distinct from competitors (Futurepedia, Toolify, There's An AI For That, Futuretools) which all lean on "tool" or "future"
- The tagline writes itself: *"Every AI. One mind."*

**Runner-up names considered and rejected:**
| Name | Why rejected |
|---|---|
| Synapse | Overused in biotech; weaker trademark |
| The Grid | Too abstract; no AI signal |
| Neural Index | Feels like a database, not a destination |
| AI Atlas | "Atlas" is everywhere in SaaS already |
| Lumen | Generic lighting/SaaS name |
| Nexus AI | "Nexus" is overused; literal but generic |

## 2. Positioning

**One-line pitch:** OMNIMIND is the curated home base for anyone navigating the AI landscape — a single nexus linking the 100 best chatbots, agents, and applications across every category, with reviews, benchmarks, and direct access.

**What OMNIMIND is NOT:**
- Not another 10,000-tool dumping ground (Futurepedia, AITopTools already own that)
- Not a submission-for-pay SEO farm
- Not a listicle blog
- Not a "use our wrapper to access 50 AIs" aggregator like GlobalGPT

**What OMNIMIND IS:**
- A **curated, opinionated, beautifully designed** discovery layer for ~100 tools that actually matter
- Organised by **use-case and capability**, not alphabetically
- Every entry has: category tag, tier (frontier / leading / specialist / open-source), one-line verdict, pricing model, direct link
- Updated monthly, not daily — quality over churn

| Competitor | Strength | Weakness we exploit |

**The gap:** nobody has combined *editorial curation + serious design craft + honest tiering*. Every existing directory looks like it was built from the same SaaS template. OMNIMIND wins on **taste**, **restraint**, and **visual identity**.

## 5. Information Architecture

```
/ (home)                      → hero, featured 6, categories grid, "editor's pick of the week"
/category/[slug]              → all tools in one category, filterable by tier and price
/tool/[slug]                  → deep dive: verdict, pricing, strengths, weaknesses, alternatives, direct link
/compare?a=claude&b=chatgpt   → side-by-side on benchmarks, price, features
/stacks                       → curated bundles ("The Designer's Stack", "The Developer's Stack")
/new                          → newly added or updated (monthly cadence)
/about                        → editorial philosophy, how we curate
/submit                       → not pay-to-play; genuine tips welcomed
```

**No login required.** Anonymous browsing is the default. Optional "save to my stack" feature later, post-launch.

## 6. Design Direction

**Aesthetic:** Editorial minimalism meets dark-mode tech magazine. Think *Pitchfork × Linear × The Browser Company*.

**Chosen direction — "Obsidian Editorial":**
- **Palette:** Near-black base (`#0A0A0B`), bone-white text (`#EDEAE4`), single accent (`#FF5E1A` — a signal orange that reads as "AI fire"), muted slate secondaries
- **Typography:**
  - **Display:** *Fraunces* (variable serif, characterful, SOFT mode) for headlines — feels editorial, not Silicon Valley
  - **Body:** *Geist* or *JetBrains Mono* for UI/metadata, *Inter Tight* for reading — NOT boring Inter
- **Grid:** Asymmetric 12-col, generous whitespace, with occasional oversized numerals as section markers
- **Motion:** Subtle reveal-on-scroll, hover-scale on tool cards, no kinetic overload
- **Details:** Noise-texture overlay (1-3% opacity), thin hairline dividers, tier badges as small iconography not text labels

**What it must NOT look like:**
- Generic purple-gradient SaaS landing page
- "12 cards in a 4-column grid on a white background" listicle
- AI-generated cyberpunk clip-art backgrounds
- Stock startup hero with glowing orbs

## 7. Tech Stack Recommendation

| Layer | Choice | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Framework | **Next.js 15 (App Router)** | Best-in-class for SEO-heavy directory |
| Styling | **Tailwind v4 + custom CSS variables** | Speed + theming |
| Content | **MDX** for tool pages, stored in repo | Git-based editorial workflow, no CMS overhead at 100 entries |
| Search | **Client-side Fuse.js** at launch; swap to Algolia when catalog >500 | No premature optimisation |
| Hosting | **Vercel** | Edge, previews, cheap at this scale |
| Analytics | **Plausible** | Privacy-first, no cookie banner |
| Fonts | **Fontshare** (Fraunces), **Vercel Fonts** (Geist) | Free, performant |
| Images | **Next/Image** + `/public` at launch | Upgrade to Cloudflare Images later |

**Why not Framer or Webflow:** both struggle at 100+ programmatically-rendered pages and lock you out of future growth.

## 8. Monetisation (post-launch, not at launch)

- **Affiliate links** where the tool offers them (Notion, Jasper, Framer, etc.) — disclosed clearly
- **Sponsored "Editor's Pick" slot** (max 1 per month, clearly labelled)
- **OMNIMIND Pro** ($5/mo): curated email, early access to new stacks, no ads
- **Never:** pay-to-list, pay-to-rank, hidden sponsorships

## 11. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|
| Catalog goes stale fast | Monthly review ritual; "Last updated" on every card |
| "Just another directory" dismissal | Lean hard into design and editorial voice from day one |
| Legal (trademarks, logos) | Use text-only wordmarks until partnerships are secured; no logo cloning |
| AI categories keep fragmenting | Quarterly IA review; categories are not fixed for all time |
| Competition from incumbents | Speed and taste — incumbents can't redesign their way out of generic-ness overnight |
