■ THE VERDICT

If you're paying for one AI, pay for this one. Claude hallucinates less, reasons more carefully, and turns long documents into something you can actually use. The coding is best-in-class; the writing is the only AI prose that doesn't read as AI prose.

Why this one.

The case for Claude, and when to reach for something else instead.

Claude is what happens when a frontier lab optimises for accuracy over spectacle. It won't wow you with an image generator or a voice demo. What it does, it does with a precision and a restraint that's rare in 2026's AI landscape.

The coding story

Opus 4.7 became the first model to break 80% on SWE-bench Verified — a benchmark of real-world GitHub issues resolved autonomously. That's not a marketing number. It's the difference between a coding assistant that writes plausible-looking code and one that actually ships working pull requests. Teams using Claude Code (the terminal agent) and Cursor (the IDE) have reported 30–50% reductions in time-to-ship on routine engineering tasks.

Artifacts — Claude's in-chat canvas for code, documents, and interactive components — turn a chat turn into something you can open, iterate on, and deploy. No other chatbot makes the chat-to-artefact loop feel this natural.

The writing story

Claude's prose has a texture that the other frontier models haven't quite matched. It knows when to stop a sentence. It doesn't default to bullet points. It handles nuance in brief analytical writing better than any model on the market. For long-form content — essays, reports, internal memos — it's the one knowledge workers keep coming back to.

Where it falls short

Claude is not the model to use if you want real-time web search, native image generation, or a voice mode to rival ChatGPT's. Anthropic has consistently prioritised depth over breadth, and the result is a tool that's excellent at a focused set of things and simply unavailable for others. If you need one AI to also generate your images and read you the news in a realistic voice, ChatGPT is still the broader pick.

The free tier is more restrictive than ChatGPT's. Heavy users will hit message limits during long sessions and want to upgrade quickly.

Who should pay for Pro

Anyone who codes. Anyone who writes for a living. Anyone who regularly analyses documents longer than 20 pages. At $20/month, Pro is a trivial expense relative to the time it saves, and the Max tier ($200/mo) with 20× the quota is the honest answer for full-time heavy users.
